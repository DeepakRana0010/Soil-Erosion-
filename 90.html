Biodiversity_and_Habitat

Biodiversity and habitat are closely related concepts in ecology. Biodiversity refers to the variety of living organisms and their interactions within an ecosystem, while habitat refers to the physical and biological environment in which an organism lives and grows.

Aralship

Habitat is critical to maintaining biodiversity because different species require different types of habitats to survive and thrive. For example, a forest ecosystem provides habitat for a wide variety of plant and animal species, each of which has unique requirements for food, shelter, and breeding. A diverse range of habitats within an ecosystem supports a diverse range of species, which in turn enhances the overall biodiversity of the ecosystem.
